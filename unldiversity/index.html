Sea of more than just red

By Reece Ristau

In December 2013, the University of Nebraska-Lincoln launched the "Not Here, Not Now, Not Ever!" campaign. After a series of race-related incidents, mainly involving the use of racial slurs on campus, 'Not Here' aimed to give minority students on campus a voice to discuss the challenges they face everyday. But just how big is the Sea of White within the Sea of Red? Read below to understand who UNL students are and where they're from.

Local draw

Chancellor Harvey Perlman set a goal last year for UNL's population to hit 30,000. With 25,006 students enrolling in 2014, the benchmark is still in the distance. The data from a local perspective, seen below, suggests that drawing students from in-state will likely not help UNL achieve its goal, as little growth has occurred. The map below compares the number of students in 2005 and 2014 from each county*, with green counties seeing positive growth and red counties seeing decline.



*In 2014, 173 students were marked as "unknown." That number was 1,139 in 2005.



Representation

Minority students represented 16 percent of the UNL population in 2014. The chart below shows changes in minority populations between 2008 and 2014. International scope

Perlman's focus on 30,000 expands beyond Nebraska's borders. In 2011, Perlman was named an Honorary University Professor of Xi'an Jiaotong University, in Xi'an, China and since then, the number of Chinese students has risen. Brazil, Saudi Arabia and Iran have also sent more students between 2005 and 2014.
